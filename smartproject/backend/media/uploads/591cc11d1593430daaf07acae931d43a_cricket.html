🏏 Cricket Drills

Master batting, bowling, fielding and fitness with drills designed for young cricketers aged 8–18.

Shadow Batting

Batting
Beginner

Practice your stance and bat swing without a ball. Focus on footwork, head position, and follow-through for each shot.

⏱ 15 min 🔄 3 sets 👤 Solo

Steps

  1. Take your batting stance in front of a mirror or open area
  2. Practice front-foot defence 20 times — focus on leading with your head
  3. Practice back-foot punch 20 times — transfer weight smoothly
  4. Play cover drive motions 20 times — ensure full bat swing
  5. Repeat pull shot, cut shot, and straight drive sequences

💡 Coach Tip

Keep your eyes level and head still. Imagine a real bowler while practising — visualisation builds muscle memory faster.

Throw-Down Hitting

Batting
Intermediate

Face throw-downs from a partner at varying pace and length. Focus on timing, shot selection, and playing straight.

⏱ 20 min 🔄 5 sets of 12 👥 Partner

Steps

  1. Set up in the nets with full batting gear
  2. Partner throws from 16 yards at moderate pace
  3. First set: play only defensive shots
  4. Second set: drive every ball on the ground
  5. Third set: mix attacking and defending naturally

💡 Coach Tip

Watch the ball from the hand. Don't pre-decide your shot — react to the length and play accordingly.

Target Bowling

Bowling
Beginner

Bowl at a target cone placed on a good length spot. Focus on run-up rhythm, release point, and consistent line.

⏱ 20 min 🔄 6 overs 👤 Solo

Steps

  1. Place a target cone on a good length at off-stump line
  2. Mark your run-up starting point
  3. Bowl 6 balls per over, aiming to hit the cone
  4. Track how many times you hit or pass close to the target
  5. Move the target to different lengths between overs

💡 Coach Tip

Consistency > speed. Focus on hitting the same spot repeatedly before increasing your pace.

Yorker Practice

Bowling
Advanced

Master the death-over yorker delivery. Practice hitting the base of the stumps consistently under pressure.

⏱ 25 min 🔄 8 overs 👤 Solo

Steps

  1. Place a shoe or towel at the batting crease as a target
  2. Bowl full run-up, aiming for full-length on the base of stumps
  3. Score yourself: 2 pts for yorker, 1 pt for full length, 0 otherwise
  4. Set a goal of 8+ points per over
  5. Practice wide yorkers on the 5th and 6th balls

💡 Coach Tip

Release the ball slightly later than normal — that's the key to a good yorker. Keep your bowling arm high.

Ground Fielding Circuit

Fielding
Beginner

Improve ground fielding with a circuit that covers pick-up-and-throw, diving stops, and underarm hits at the stumps.

⏱ 15 min 🔄 4 rounds 👥 Team

Steps

  1. Set up 4 stations in a square: attack, pick-up, dive, throw
  2. Station 1: Charge at the ball, pick up on the move and throw
  3. Station 2: Field a rolling ball off your weaker side
  4. Station 3: Dive to stop a wide throw, recover, and throw
  5. Station 4: Underarm hit the stumps from 10 metres

💡 Coach Tip

Get low — bent knees and soft hands are the foundation of great fielding. Always watch the ball into your hands.

High Catch Training

Fielding
Intermediate

Practice taking high catches with a partner using a sling or high throws. Build confidence under pressure catches.

⏱ 15 min 🔄 20 catches 👥 Partner

Steps

  1. Partner hits or throws the ball high into the air
  2. Call for the catch loudly ("Mine!")
  3. Position under the ball early, fingers pointing up
  4. Cushion the ball with soft hands at chest height
  5. Practice with the sun behind to simulate match conditions

💡 Coach Tip

Never take your eyes off the ball. Keep your head still and let the ball come to you — don't snatch at it.

Cricket Shuttle Runs

Fitness
Intermediate

Build match-specific fitness with shuttle runs simulating quick singles, doubles, and sprint finishes between wickets.

⏱ 12 min 🔄 6 sets 👤 Solo

Steps

  1. Mark 17.68m (pitch length) with cones at each end
  2. Set 1: Run 2 singles (1 run × 2) — turn fast, slide bat
  3. Set 2: Run 2 doubles (2 runs × 2) — focus on turning speed
  4. Set 3: Run a triple — sprint all the way through
  5. Rest 30 seconds between sets, repeat 6 times

💡 Coach Tip

Turn like a batsman — dip your shoulder, slide the bat, and explode out of the turn. This builds real match fitness.

Interval Sprint Training

Fitness
Advanced

High-intensity sprint intervals to build explosive speed for quick singles and boundary chases in the field.

⏱ 20 min 🔄 8 sets 👤 Solo

Steps

  1. Mark 30m, 60m, and 100m distances
  2. Sprint 30m at 100% effort — walk back for recovery
  3. Sprint 60m at 90% effort — jog back slowly
  4. Sprint 100m at 85% effort — 90s rest
  5. Complete 8 total sets alternating distances

💡 Coach Tip

Pump your arms aggressively and stay on your toes. Proper sprint form in training translates to faster running in matches.
